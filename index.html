About Me

I am an Information Technology student at the Harare Institute of Technology where I am one of the university’s most recognised student voices, twice selected as a speaker at the HIT graduation ceremonies.

My work lives at the intersection of data, software, community and advocacy. I build solutions and tell stories that centre the people most often left out of the conversation—creating technology that works for all.

Through my internship in Software Development at the Africa Clinical Research Network I am growing my understanding of software, contributing to building systems that support healthcare delivery across Africa.Not only that through the Women Techsters Fellowship (Data Science and Engineering by Tech4Dev), I am deepening technical expertise in Python, Power BI, SQL and end-to-end data workflows while my roles at Enactus HIT, She STEMs Foundation, and GDG on Campus ensure that expertise stays tethered to the communities it should serve.

“I don’t just want to work in tech. I want to ensure that when Africa tells its own story through data, that story is told with precision, with dignity and with power.So that they influence African problem solvers to build solutions that actually work for the continent.”

I hold the McKinsey Forward Certificate, Google Data Analytics Certificate, and am pursuing additional credentials in emerging tech fields. I’m committed to continuous learning and using technology for social impact.
