About Me

I am an Information Technology student at the Harare Institute of Technology where I am one of the university's most recognised student voices, twice selected as a keynote speaker at institutional events, and an active member of Enactus HIT where I lead innovation projects centred on social impact.

My work lives at the intersection of data, software, community and advocacy. I build solutions and tell stories that centre the people most often left out of the conversation — ensuring that community voices are heard and that technology serves those who need it most.

Through my internship in Software Development at the Africa Clinical Research Network I am growing my understanding of software and contributing to building systems that support critical research across the continent. I'm particularly passionate about healthcare technology and data systems that improve outcomes for underserved populations.

"I don't just want to work in tech. I want to ensure that when Africa tells its own story through data, that story is told with precision, with dignity and with power."

I hold the McKinsey Forward Certificate and a Google Data Analytics Certificate, and I am currently completing additional professional courses to strengthen my foundation in machine learning and cloud infrastructure.
